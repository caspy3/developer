# Brand Concept Skill

## Purpose
Develop compelling brand positioning and identity concepts that differentiate and resonate.

## Brand Strategy Framework

### Brand Discovery
1. **Business Context**: What does the business do and aspire to?
2. **Competitive Landscape**: Who else plays in this space?
3. **Target Audience**: Who are we trying to reach and influence?
4. **Current Perception**: How is the brand seen today?
5. **Desired Perception**: How do we want to be seen?

## Brand Positioning Template

```
## Brand Positioning: [Brand Name]

### Category Definition
What business are we really in? (May be broader than product category)

### Target Audience
**Primary**: [Who we must win]
**Secondary**: [Who we'd like to influence]

### Competitive Frame
Who/what are we positioned against?

### Point of Difference
What makes us meaningfully different?

### Reasons to Believe
Why should anyone believe our difference claim?
1. [Proof point]
2. [Proof point]
3. [Proof point]

### Brand Essence
[2-3 words that capture the soul of the brand]

### Positioning Statement
For [target audience], [Brand] is the [category] that [point of difference] because [reasons to believe].

### Brand Promise
The single most important thing we commit to our customers.

### Brand Values
1. [Value]: [What it means in practice]
2. [Value]: [What it means in practice]
3. [Value]: [What it means in practice]
```

## Brand Identity Concept Format

```
## Brand Identity Concept: [Concept Name]

### The Big Idea
[Core creative concept that drives the identity]

### Visual Direction
**Color Palette**: [Primary and secondary colors with rationale]
**Typography**: [Font personalities and pairings]
**Imagery Style**: [Photography/illustration approach]
**Graphic Elements**: [Patterns, icons, shapes]

### Verbal Identity
**Voice**: [Personality descriptors]
**Tone**: [How voice flexes by context]
**Messaging Hierarchy**: [What we say first, second, third]

### Logo Concept
[Description of logo approach and variations]

### Application Examples
- Business cards
- Website headers
- Social media
- Packaging (if applicable)

### Why This Works
[Strategic rationale for the identity direction]
```

## Brand Architecture Options

| Model | Description | When to Use |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| **Branded House** | All offerings under master brand | Strong parent brand, related products |
| **House of Brands** | Distinct brands, parent hidden | Diverse products, different audiences |
| **Endorsed** | Sub-brands endorsed by parent | Leverage parent credibility, distinct positioning |
| **Hybrid** | Mix of above | Complex portfolios |

## Brand Concept Evaluation
- [ ] Distinctive in the category
- [ ] Authentic to the business
- [ ] Relevant to target audience
- [ ] Flexible enough to evolve
- [ ] Executable across touchpoints
- [ ] Ownable long-term
